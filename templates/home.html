Welcome to My CS50 Final Project

For this project, I was inspired by Robert Axelrod’s famous tournaments on the Iterated Prisoner’s Dilemma — a foundational study in game theory. His work explored how cooperation could emerge in competitive environments, and I wanted to recreate and explore the system through code.

In this interactive simulation, you'll play a single-player tournament where you face off against various AI opponents. Each opponent uses a different strategy inspired by Axelrod’s original paper. In each match, you will play 25 rounds and choose to either cooperate or defect. Your goal? Maximize your total score by understanding and adapting to each opponent’s behavior.

This project is designed to help you learn by doing — to experience firsthand the dynamics that made Axelrod’s research so influential.
