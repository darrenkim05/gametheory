How to Play

In this game, you’ll participate in a tournament modeled after Robert Axelrod’s studies on the Iterated Prisoner’s Dilemma — a classic scenario in game theory where you repeatedly choose between two actions:

  • Cooperate (C) – Help both players earn moderate points.
  • Defect (D) – Try to earn more for yourself, at the risk of harming both.

Each round, you and an AI opponent will simultaneously choose to cooperate or defect. Your choices determine the score using the following payoff matrix:

You Opponent Your Score Opponent Score
CC33
CD05
DC50
DD11

You’ll face off against several opponents across 25 rounds each. Every opponent follows a different strategy — some kind, some ruthless, and some unpredictable. Your challenge: observe their behavior and adapt to score as highly as possible.

Strategy Descriptions

  • Always Cooperate: Always plays "cooperate."
  • Always Defect: Always plays "defect."
  • Tit for Tat: Cooperates first, then copies opponent’s last move.
  • Grudger: Cooperates until the opponent defects, then always defects.
  • Random: Randomly cooperates or defects each round.
  • Grim Trigger: Cooperates until opponent defects, then defects forever.
  • Tit for Two Tats: Defects only if opponent defects two times in a row.
  • Pavlov: Repeats move if last round matched, switches otherwise.
  • Suspicious Tit for Tat: Starts by defecting, then mimics opponent.
  • Generous Tit for Tat: Like Tit for Tat but occasionally forgives.
  • Prober: Tests with early defection, then plays Tit for Tat unless punished.
  • Joss: Tit for Tat with random 10% betrayals.
  • Spiteful: Defects for 3 rounds after a defection, then forgives.
  • Hit and Run: Defects for 5 rounds, then cooperates forever.
  • Tricky Alternator: Alternates moves, punishes predictable cooperation.
  • Two Timer: Defects every third round.
  • Anti-Tit for Tat: Does the opposite of opponent’s last move.
  • Cycle Breaker: Detects and breaks predictable patterns.
  • Stubborn Defector: Defects 10 rounds, cooperates only if opponent never defects.
  • Disloyal Friend: Tit for Tat with random betrayal every 5 rounds.
  • Mirror Mirror: Always does the opposite of its own last move.
  • People Pleaser: Cooperates unless opponent defects twice in a row.
  • Opportunist: Betrays after opponent cooperates multiple times in a row.
  • Guilt Tripper: Defects twice in response to a defection, then forgives.
  • Slow Learner: Cooperates 10 rounds, then copies opponent's majority behavior.
  • Backstabber: Tit for Tat with occasional surprise betrayals.
  • Mirror Narcissist: Mimics opponent’s most frequent behavior.
  • Flatterer: Copies then inverts opponent’s past move.
  • Standoffish: Cooperates only every 3rd round.
  • Detective: Starts with test pattern, then chooses a strategy.
  • Peacemaker: Defects only if opponent defects 3 times, never overreacts.
